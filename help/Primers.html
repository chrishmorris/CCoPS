Designing Primers with PLan

This page helps you design primers for amplifying a target sequence.

Providing the target sequence

You can provide the DNA sequence alone, or a FASTA representation of it. You provide it in one of the following ways.

  • Paste the text into the box on the page.
  • (Soon) Drag the file from a folder and drop it the page.
  • (Soon) Click the button and open the file.
  • (Soon) Choose a database and provide the accession number.

(Optional) Specifying PCR conditions

There is a form defining the PCR conditions you intend to use. Clcik where it says "PCR Conditions" to see it. This is used to calculate the Tm of the primers. The defaults are often appropriate, but you can also change them. Please contact us if you want us to include other options in this form.

Changing start, end, and length of primers

When you have supplied the target sequence, PLan will suggest primer sequences suitable for amplifying the full length sequence. If you change the start or end codon, then it will recalculate them.

The suggested primers have a melting temperature greater that the minimum Tm you requested, but no more than 5C above it. If possible, they also end in C or G. To lengthen a primer, click on it. To shorten a primer, right click on it.

Designing extra primers

To add another forward or reverse primer for a given target, click "+", then set the start or end codon.

Designing primers for several targets

If you supply a FASTA file representing several targets, the table will contain primers for all of them.

These primers are all designed using the same conditions, so it will be possible to perform the PCR processes together.

Potential problems in PCR

For each primer, PLan reports the Tm of a possible hairpin. If you put your mouse over this, PLan will display the alignment of the hairpin.

For each target, PLan reports the Tm of a possible primer dimer. If you have designed more than two primers, the number reported is the highest Tm of any pair of a forward primer and reverse primer. If you put your mouse over this, PLan will display the alignment of the primer dimer.

The annealing temperature that you use should be higher than the worst hairpin or primer dimer in the reactions you are performing.

Saving

Click "". The page will be saved as a .eml file. You can open this file in your email program, or in MS Word. If you change the extension to ".xls" then you can also open it with MS Excel.

Future work

Later, PLan will support the design of mutagenic primers, and also allow you to upload details of your primers and record where they are in your freezers.
